Feedback:

Hi Camilla Good work all the right elements are there in terms of composition and content. I like the white minimalist approach it usually leads to professional designs. The red floating Book button is quite a nice idea. Nice job on the google maps very cool. ok some thoughts from me. Code: Your class names for CSS you should rather opt for kebab case names instead of CamelCase. change: vigimgTwo to vigimg-two oprahTextTwo to oprah-text-two When writing CSS in media queries, try to achieve the same result with the least amount of code as you can possibly get away with. You wrote 828 lines of CSS. I am sure can make this less. Design Some of the elements on the homepage are too close to each other, have a look at my screen shots. look at the screen shot of www.getyourguide.com they have a grid like yours, however pay close attention to the white space between the text and the images and the white space between the titles and sub titles they are all consistent. please notice in my one screen shot I have marked in red... the logo and the first title and the map are left aligned and. on the right side the butt end of the navigation is right aligned with the map box and the book button. You must create visual symmetry with your elements, everything must line up perfectly if creating grids and lines. Good job, you really improve with every project. Look at some design patterns https://uigarage.net/platform/web/ notice that footer text font size is usually much smaller than the rest of the page. This creates a visual hierarchy from large to small text top to bottom.
